TITLE: TAMU-CC Time Off and Leave Overview
TAMU-CC provides regular, budgeted, leave-eligible employees with paid time off options such as vacation, sick leave, holidays, family and medical leave, and birthday leave. 
These benefits are designed to help employees balance work, personal, and family life.

Employees can refer to the official HR website for complete and current policies:
- Time Off Program Guide: https://www.tamucc.edu/human-resources/time-off/assets/documents/time-off-guide.pdf
- Holiday Schedule: https://www.tamucc.edu/human-resources/time-off/holiday.php
- Birthday Leave: https://www.tamucc.edu/human-resources/time-off/birthday-leave.php
- Family and Medical Leave (FMLA): https://www.tamucc.edu/human-resources/time-off/fmla.php

The University observes the following legal holidays:
New Year’s Day, Memorial Day, Independence Day, Labor Day, Thanksgiving, and Christmas Day.

Employees may be eligible for additional time-off programs such as bereavement leave, voting leave, jury duty leave, and emergency leave.



TITLE: Birthday Leave Summary
TAMU-CC offers paid time off for employees’ birthdays as a recognition benefit.
- Must be taken within 40 days before or after the birthday.
- Not eligible for payout.
- Full-time employees: 8 hours | Part-time employees: proportional.
- Cannot be split into partial days.
- Must be approved by the supervisor.



TITLE: Vacation and Sick Leave Summary
Vacation:
- Accrued monthly based on years of service.
- Carry-forward maximums range from 180 to 532 hours.
- Must have 6 months of continuous state employment before using vacation leave.

Sick Leave:
- 8 hours per month for full-time employees.
- Proportional for part-time employees.
- Unlimited accumulation; unused hours carry forward annually.

TITLE: Detailed Birthday Leave Procedure
Eligible employees must request birthday leave through Workday:
1. Log in at https://sso.tamus.edu
2. Select “Time Off” worklet → “Time Off”
3. Choose the day of leave → Select “Other Emergencies” as Type
4. Enter “Birthday Leave” in the comment box.
Approval by the supervisor is mandatory before leave is taken.
Supervisors must ensure that the employee has not already used their birthday leave for the fiscal year.

If an employee is not physically at work during their birthday month (due to sick leave, FMLA, LWOP, etc.), the leave must be taken within 30 days of returning to work.



TITLE: Holiday Pay and Eligibility
Employees must be in a paid status for at least part of:
- The last regularly scheduled workday before the holiday, and
- The first regularly scheduled workday after the holiday,
to receive holiday pay.

Employees who work on a scheduled holiday are entitled to equivalent time off within 12 months following the holiday week.

Supervisors may deny vacation leave requests if granting them causes operational hardship.



TITLE: Family and Medical Leave (FMLA) Essentials
- Up to 4 weeks of job-protected leave per year for qualifying medical or family reasons.
- Must have 32 months of employment and 2200 hours worked.
- Runs concurrently with paid leave (vacation/sick).
- Medical certification must be submitted within 15 days of request.
- Return-to-work clearance may be required.

TITLE: Sick Leave Pool and Donation Process
Employees must exhaust all personal leave before applying.
Required documents:
- Medical Certification Form
- Sick Leave Pool Withdrawal Form
All forms go through HR for review and must be approved before additional hours are granted.
Retroactive requests are not allowed.

Donation Tax Guidelines:
- Donations under 40 hours (non-medical emergency) are taxable.
- Donations over 40 hours for medical emergencies are non-taxable.



TITLE: Workers’ Compensation Internal Workflow
Supervisors must submit First Report of Injury through Origami within 24 hours.
Email notification to payroll@tamucc.edu is mandatory after submission.
Department heads must ensure compliance and forward required medical documentation for state reporting.
