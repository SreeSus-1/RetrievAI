CATEGORY: PUBLIC
==============================

TITLE: IT Acceptable Use Summary
The IT Acceptable Use Standard defines rights and responsibilities for all users of information and information resources owned or controlled by Texas A&M University–Corpus Christi (TAMU-CC).

The purpose of this policy is to ensure the responsible use of university information technology systems and to mitigate risks related to information security, privacy, and accessibility.

This policy applies to:
- All faculty, staff, students, affiliates, contractors, and guests.
- All information resources such as computers, servers, networks, and cloud systems under TAMU-CC control.

Users are expected to:
- Protect university data.
- Use technology for authorized purposes only.
- Respect others’ privacy and intellectual property.

For official references:
- Security of Electronic Information Resources (Rule 29.01.99.C1)  
  https://www.tamucc.edu/governance/rules-procedures/assets/29.01.99.c1security-of-electronic-information-resources.pdf
- TAMU System Data Classification Standard  
  https://assets.system.tamus.edu/files/policy/pdf/securitystandards/



TITLE: Purpose and Audience
The goal of this standard is to educate all users about their rights and responsibilities in using TAMU-CC information resources.  
The audience includes faculty, staff, students, affiliates, and contractors.



TITLE: Key Definitions (Public Overview)
- **Public Information:** Data intentionally available to the general public through official websites or media.  
- **Confidential Information:** Data exempt from public disclosure under state or federal law (e.g., student records, SSNs, health information).  
- **Information Resources:** Equipment, software, and procedures used to collect, process, store, and transmit university information.

CATEGORY: INTERNAL
==============================

TITLE: Roles and Responsibilities (Internal Summary)
TAMU-CC defines several key IT roles:

- **Owner:** Person or department authorized to determine who may access a resource.  
- **Custodian:** Individual or team responsible for maintaining systems and implementing security controls defined by the Owner.  
- **Information Security Officer (ISO):** Responsible for university-wide information security.  
- **Information Resources Manager (IRM):** Designated by the President, manages all information resources.  
- **EIR Accessibility Coordinator (EIRAC):** Ensures that digital content meets accessibility standards (TAC 213).  

Employees in these roles must maintain documentation of access privileges and review them annually.



TITLE: Controlled Information Guidelines
Controlled information includes:
- Operational information, personnel records, internal communications, research data, and IT security procedures.  
Such information is not intended for public release but may be disclosed through proper channels if required by law.

Access to controlled information must follow university authentication and authorization policies, including password management and multifactor authentication (MFA).



TITLE: System Usage and Enforcement
- Users must not share credentials, download unauthorized software, or attempt to bypass security mechanisms.
- System logs are monitored for security and compliance.
- Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action, suspension of access, or referral to HR or legal authorities.

TITLE: Incident Response Workflow (Private Access)
1. User identifies or suspects a security incident (phishing, malware, data loss).
2. Report immediately via the IT Service Desk or security@tamucc.edu.
3. ISO classifies the incident and notifies the IRM.
4. The response team performs containment, eradication, and recovery.
5. Final reports are submitted to system administration and legal counsel if required.

Confidential data (student records, financial data, SSNs, etc.) must be encrypted during all stages of handling.



TITLE: Vendor and Contractor Access
Contractors and third parties with an IslandID must:
- Sign the Acceptable Use Agreement.
- Complete the annual security awareness module.
- Use TAMU-CC-approved VPN for remote access.
- Follow data retention and disposal guidelines.

Access credentials are terminated immediately upon contract completion or violation of university IT policies.
