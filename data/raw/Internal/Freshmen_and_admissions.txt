TITLE: First-Time Freshmen – Definition
First-time freshmen are students who will graduate or have graduated from high school or received a GED AND have not enrolled or completed 24 transferable hours at another institution after high school or earning a GED.

---

TITLE: High School Diploma Requirements
Students may be considered for admission by submitting a complete application if they graduate with one of the following diploma types:

- Distinguished Achievement (HB5)
- Recommended (UAP)
- Distinguished (UAP)

Required credits for first-time college students:
- 4 English
- 4 Math
- 4 Science
- 3 Social Studies
- 2 credits of the same Foreign Language

Homeschooled, Out-of-State, and Private School students are exempt from the diploma and credit requirements.

---

TITLE: Getting Started – How to Become an Islander
For help, contact your admissions counselor:  
Phone: 361-825-7024  
Email: recruitment@tamucc.edu

Students meeting class rank and diploma requirements may be guaranteed admission.  
Students from non-ranking high schools receive a calculated rank based on GPA.

Programs such as Nursing, Engineering, Graphic Design, Music, and Theatre require additional admission steps after acceptance.

---

TITLE: Admission by Class Rank
1st & 2nd Quarter  
- Guaranteed Admission  
- Test scores optional but encouraged  

3rd & 4th Quarter  
- Holistic Review  
- Considers test scores, GPA, class rank  
- Students may receive Conditional Admission if outside automatic admission

---

TITLE: Application Process – Step-by-Step
STEP ONE: Review Admission Requirements

STEP TWO: Complete ApplyTexas Application  
Essay is not required.

STEP THREE: Pay Application Fee  
- $40 for U.S. students  
- $50 for late applications  
Fee waivers accepted: ACT, SAT, NACAC, or official hardship letters.

STEP FOUR: Submit Official Transcript(s)  
Electronic transcripts via: Naviance, TREx, Parchment, Clearinghouse → admiss@tamucc.edu  
Paper transcripts (sealed) mailed to:

Texas A&M University–Corpus Christi  
Office of Recruitment and Admissions  
6300 Ocean Drive, Unit 5774  
Corpus Christi, TX 78412-5774

If college coursework was taken, official college transcripts are also required.

---

TITLE: Test Scores (ACT/SAT & TSI)
ACT/SAT scores are not required but strongly encouraged for:
- Scholarships
- Placement
- TSI exemptions

Codes:  
SAT: 0366  
ACT: 4045

TITLE: Application Deadlines
Fall Semester – July 1 (late fee begins)  
Spring Semester – November 1  
Summer Semester – April 1  

---

TITLE: Freshman Scholarship Deadlines
Priority Deadline: October 1  
Final Deadline: February 1  

---

TITLE: After Acceptance – Next Steps
• Submit Bacterial Meningitis vaccination record through Med+Proctor  
• Complete FAFSA  
• Review Islander Housing (required for first-year students under age 21 living outside the Coastal Bend)  
• Submit TSI scores or exemption documentation  

---

TITLE: Additional Admissions Programs
Conditional Admission  
Structured support for students outside automatic requirements.

College Connection Program (CCP)  
Del Mar College co-enrollment program with automatic transfer upon requirements.

Islander Academy  
Dual-credit program for high school students.

Program for System Admission (PSA)  
Pathway via other Texas A&M System institutions with transfer opportunity later.
